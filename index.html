Online Research Center for ESC Rights Monitoring

As part of the Practicum for my International Affairs Master’s degree, I was part of a team of fellow M.A. candidates that did consulting work for the Center for Economic and Social Rights. CESR was in need of a one-stop-shop for all things related to Economic, Social and Cultural rights for themselves and all interested organizations to access. Our team proposed an online resource center for CESR to host all of its own resources and data as well as resources from related organizations and institutions.

We created a 70-page best practice document to pass off to a design team complete with design frameworks, organizational hierarchies, competitor assessments, and content recommendations. HPCA Palliative Care SMS Messaging System

This is a project that was completed as part of partnership between the Open Societies Foundation and Parsons the New School for Design. OSF designated partner organizations for teams of students to work with to tackle an issue the organization was facing with technological solutions. In our case we worked with the Hospice Palliative Care Association of South Africa who was in need of support for their training program designed to teach palliative (end-of-life) care to traditional healers in South Africa.

In response to this, our team designed an SMS messaging system that the traditional healers could use as a follow-up to their training. The messaging system allows the traditional healers to text in special codes that correspond to a variety of issues and ailments their patients may be facing that require palliative care techniques and receive instructions on what to do in those situations. This system was designed to be available in several local languages, beginning first with English and Zulu. Below you can find the posters we designed for the traditional healers to have up in their practices. The information on these posters were also converted into small pamphlets that traditional healers could carry with them on house calls or anywhere they needed to.
